Synthesizing
Synthesizing
We met a huge challenge when trying to synthesize our interview results through affinity mapping: the number of affinity notes from the 3 stakeholers were highly unbalanced, for we only had 2 administrators for our program
We eventually chose to synthesize all stakeholers‚Äô affinity notes together because I realized the magic of affinity mapping in helping discover surprising connections. The key insights from affinity mapping are:
  • Everyone is interested in the next stage (career path, company & industry, location)
  • There are 2 kinds of information seekers:
  • Introvert analytical thinker who would collect information and do their own research
  • Proactive coffee chat lover who prefer to reach out to alumni directly
  • Alumni are particularly willing to share their career move data to the community but also care about how much effort and time they need to commit
  • Alumni are willing to give back but want to find meaning in it

After conducting a feedback session with current students and program administrators, I decided to move forward with the Timeline View. With no existing design patterns for reference, I had a lot to consider and needed to design for every edge cases:

1. The length of path is proportional to its time span. If the length of certian path exceeds the horizontal space, a horizontal scrollbar would be enabled

2. Every timeline viz end in current experience level by defualt, instead of starting at MS-HCI experience

3. Deeper colors, higher levels (with accessbility guidelines in mind)

4. Need to include alumni‚Äôs working experience before joining the program

5. Need to include internship experience during program study

9. Only display full-time job to not make the viz too messy
