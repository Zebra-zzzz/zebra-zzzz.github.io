Diary Study

We recruited 3 families with children respectively aged 5, 6 and 7, and ask them to take notes of their schedule after school / work (Nov 9, 2018). Each of them were compensated with an ￥40 meal coupon at a nearby cafe for their time.

Their notes show that they rarely have long-time communication in the evening, and 20:30 – 21:30 is the time period during which they both have respective leisure activity. We suppose that this would be a great time for them to do something together and communicate.

Observation

We went to Beijing Xinhua Bookstore and Hamleys located in Wangfujing (Nov 18, 2018) to observe children around 6-9 read books and play with toys.

We found the comic & picture book section had lots of children (mainly 5-10 years old) gatherin garound. The average time they spent there is about 50 minutes. We also checked the bestsellers for children around 7-10: children’s literature , science books, coding for child etc. These are the books that parents buy most for their children.

In Hamsley, there are plenty of boys assembling LEGO pieces to build cars or architectures, while girls mainly make some items with light clay.

An interesting fact is that there were many demonstration areas in Hamsley for children to play with toys, but only the LEGO area had a lot of parents and children gathering together. Those parents played with their children assmeblingLEGO bricks and they had frequent communications. In other areas children just playe alone.

analysis

Why LEGO is the most popular in Hamsley?
— Limitations of current toys and games

Traditionally, when players start to play a new game or toy, the game elements and items have been produced or setted in advance, so their play is actually the process of permutations and combinations of their interaction with preset game elements. As a result, their playing experience is limited and can hardly benefit creativity and foster engagement.

However, a complete game experience not only inculdes the interacting with game element period but also the game element creating period. Creating game elements by themselves at first will significantly increase the gameplay and help to achieve a customized and free game experience. They will have opportunites to create whatever they want. Additionally, they have more time to discuss with their friends or family.

The characterastic of LEGO is that even the basic LEGO bricks can be built to any game items one could imagine, so LEGO is one of the suitable option that can be applied to the game elements creating period. Besides, LEGO is welcome among all ages: children can build simple models with LEGO, while adults can also create complex objects with LEGO.

Interactive Game Carpet Part

What Game Format to Promote Communications?
— Electronic Board game

Board game is one of the most natural ways to gather people together and ensures them a great atmosphere to communicate freely.

Moreover, a game carpet with video games of different ranges projected onto enables them to enjoy plenty of games within only one game carpet. In addition, with data computing and clouds, they could play some games with complex game mechanics.

What Interation to Help Escape from the Virual World?
— Tangible Interaction

Physical items could hepl children to feel and understand the real world.

Embed some sensors in game carpet to encourage children to use some invisible things (light, temperature, etc.) in real world to interact with carpets.

What Item to Cultivate Creativity?
— LEGO Bricks

LEGO is affordable in most families, and even very simple LEGO pieces could be assembled to items, buildings, and even characters.

Help to develop children’s creativity, perception of space, as well as memory.

Iteration

This design still limits children’ s playing area to a tabletop system. With the goal of encouraging children’ s physical activity, I turned to papers for help. I eventually came to the conclusion that room-scale blended reality stimulates active engagement in the physical world, even enhancing their understanding of the physical phenomenon. So, I designed a second way of interaction: export game elements to the physical world, and then Lego models act as representations of them. Connect LEGO with sensors, and the game elements in the virtual world will change in terms of the conditions of the physical environment. Such interaction can help expand the playing environment to room-scale even world-scale. Every corner in the world can become a trigger in the game.
