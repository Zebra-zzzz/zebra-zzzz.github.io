NPS Monitoring Platform

Driving the UX iteration of an Enterprise product that monitors and analyzes net promoter score (NPS) of 19 retail touchpoints

From Inefficient to efficient

Enabling regional or store managers to effortlessly spot the biggest issues that contribute to the low NPS of a particular touchpoint, gain actionable insights, and create metrics for the corresponding department

The 1st Draft

The 1st draft didn’t provide actionable insights, because:
1. the driving factors are hidden in the tooltip, only available when you hover over a process stage
2. In order to find out the biggest issues, the store manager needs to do the calculation himself. For example, multiple the contribution of the process stage by the contribution of the driving factor

My Iterated Design

1. A responsible party tag before the driving factor to let the store manager quickly find out who should he reach out to
2. A performance status indicator, marked the highest 5 and lowest 5 driving factors, and also those in the progress of improvement
3. The color-coded performance score of each driving factor calculated by a linear regression model

From unfocused to tailored

While the original T-NPS (Transactional NPS) dashboard offers valuable insights for regional or store managers on a weekly basis. The 2 end-users actually have distinct information goals and information retrieving habits. So I broke down all of the NPS data metrics offeredby the backend, reorganized and represented them in 2 new dashboards. Each new dashboard is dedicated to only one of these 2 end-users, that, together with the original T-NPS dashboard, could support their full workflow on the platform

Need real-time operation monitoring data for each touchpoint
& weekly analytical inforamtion for each touchpoint management purpose
Need monthly high-level insights into customers’ overall satisfaction across touchpoints
& weekly analytical information for each touchpoint and store management purpose

But even for the existing T-NPS dashboard, although it tried to cater both the store managers and regional mangers’ weekly info needs at the same time, their workflows are still different. So I also made some adjustment to satisfy their respective needs (to always prioritize the info that matters most to them)

From disjointed to seamless

Some of the tasks on our end-users’ workflow related to customer experience and NPS were not supported by the 1st draft. For example, they can only summarize the success story manually, on doc or email or in in-person meeting, which will hinder the promotion of the standardized operational procedure among the organization, which is an essential needs of the executives and regional managers

What is the standardized operational procedure?

It is a guideline that provides operational rules for each driving factors. For example, in the Car Delivery touchpoints, there is a driving factor that is customized delivery ceremony, and the organization will provide some standardized operational rules like: to prepare a handwritten card and give it to the customer in the ceremony

The ideal standardized operational procedure building process

Through my contextual interview with a store manager, I summarized an ideal building process of the standardized operation procedure:

Right now, because a lot of these communications between stores, regions, and the executives are all done in external tools and all over the places like emails, docs, phone calls... It is hard to promote this standardized procedure

So in the iteration design, I tried to incorporate these processes into our platform to deliver a seamless experience:

Store manager added a regional experimental rule to improve the performance of the “Customized delivery ceremony” driving factor

1 month later, store manager verified the regional experimental rule to be effective

Regional manger tracks the implementation progress of standardized rules, and manages the regional experimental & store self-initiated rules
